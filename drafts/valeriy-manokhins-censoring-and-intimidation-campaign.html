Valeriy: a history of bullying and one-sided views on data science

Valeriy is a prolific poster on social media. He writes, amongst other things, about conformal prediction, a topic on which he did his PhD.

I had noticed since a long time that he tends to have a provocative behavior. He writes statement such as “[major ML library] misleads users” if the corresponding library does not implement conformal prediction, or will call research papers by ML major researchers (more than me) “murky” and “dubious” when they do not match his world view.

Trolling – We are all human beings, with our biases and cognitive dissonance, however Valeriy pushes provocation very far and reaches a large number of followers across various social media platforms. To this large audience, he serves simplified and misleading messages that are not helpful.

Bullying – In addition, he is often dismissive of work that does not align with his views, calling classic statistical methods or ML software all sorts of bad words (eg “19th Century” or “surface-level hacks”). He has been doing this repeatedly over years, targeting particularly the most popular tools, and thus scikit-learn (in which I am personally invested). Such attacks are harmful both to the mental well-being of the people doing the work (often volunteers, or people going the extra mile beyond their day job to democratize data science) and to the corresponding projects, which suffer for bad optics. People invest their energy in data science if it brings positive vibes out, not to see turf wars bringing being dismissive of standard tools.

An insincere conversation – Making matters worse, Valeriy systematically deletes information from Internet. He has a pattern of posting inflammatory remarks, and coming back later to remove them. As a result, it is not possible to portray well his behavior or confront.

My trigger: a disrepectful Linkedin exchange

End of August 2025, Valeriy got annoyed when watching a video of an invited lecture that I had given at the Isaac Newton Institute for Mathematical Science in Cambridge. My presentation was on uncertainty quantification, but I quickly brushed away conformal prediction [1], which wasn’t my topic.

[1]Looking at the video, I specifically said “in my experience, a lot of the tools don’t work, or they work in theory but I don’t know how to use them. One example is conformal prediction. I mean, there is some conformal prediction on classifiers, I’ve just never been able to do anything meaningful with it. The idea of coverage set when you have two outcomes, I just can’t do something useful with it.” I said this because we had tried to start from conformal theory for the work that I was presenting, but failed. I was not reading from a text with controled words, just giving an hour-long lecture.

Valeriy wrote a inflammatory LinkedIn post lashing at scikit-learn (though my lecture and my work was completely independent of scikit-learn) as such:

Scikit-learn has become an antique museum piece in machine learning. It is still paraded around as if it were modern, but in reality it lags far behind. […]

When confronted with conformal prediction, a scikit-learn representative dismissed it as useless because of sets. That statement says everything: it’s not conformal prediction that fails, it’s scikit-learn’s inability to move beyond toy-level tutorials.

He did not link to the video, not did he ever name me or was explicit about what he was referring to. But shared a screenshot of the moment at which I said the above.

His post also referred Venn-Abers conformalization of classifiers as a solution to working beyond solely two outcomes, in classification settings


I was pointed to his post, and added a comment pointing out that most conformal prediction work, an in particular the Venn Abers, had marginal control, while for decision making, as in my talk, we needed conditional control… Basically a technical discussion. I also added a link to the video (so that people could make their own opinion). Valeriy and I had a short exchange, but I noticed that he was deleting some of my comments (and linkedin leaves no traces when this happens), and he eventually deleted all my comments.


The deletion of my comments, which were not phrased in an aggressive way, got me angry, in particular since he was criticizing a work but making sure that readers could not access the corresponding work to build their own opinion. Thus I wrote a linkedin post to call out this behind. I accused him of being “toxic and dishonest”, called for people to unfollow him. I replied to Valeriy attack on scikit-learn:

Scikit-learn is free, built by people who are mostly volunteers, or people payed way below their market value because they care about making data science accessible and better. Attacks as the one below are harmful, including because they emotionally harm contributors. IMHO, they qualify as harassement. Valeriy does this repeatedly. I commented only this time because it concerned a talk that I had given.

Typically, the correct answer to Internet trolls is to ignore them because reacting amplifies them. However I decided to speak out because Valeriy has a lot of followers, more than I have.


My post received a lot of traction. Many people commented on the post, or privately, to share that they had experienced similar difficulties with Valeriy and were happy that someone spoke out.

Censoring via deletion of content and regulatory escalation

Cease-and-desist attorney letter On Sept 23rd 2025, I received a letter by French attorneys that asked to 1) remove the Linkedin post within 48 hours 2), publish a post inverting my claims and recognizing that I was wrong, 3) pay 2000 € within 8 days. The letter was strongly worded accusing me of libel (“diffamation publique”), citing legal texts, and threatening me of a lawsuit.

Do not yield to intimidation letters

The letter was factually incorrectly: it claimed that Valeriy had lost 35k followers because of my message. I would have been very proud of such success, but it was not true. A closer look at the letter with the help of an attorney and a para-legal revealed that it could be characterized as an extortion letter, and I have filled a complain to the Paris bar.

Thus, I ignored the letter.


LinkedIn Regulatory Escalation A month later, LinkedIn removed my post. Valeriy had filed a complain with them, and they took down my post without any contradictory hearing. I mentioned that the analysis of my counsels was that I would win a lawsuit. They told me to file a lawsuit…


So Valeriy managed to erase from Internet my messages trying to reply to his one-sided view of data-science and derogatory comments on tools that do not glorify his favorite techniques.

Hence, I am writing this post, to document Valeriy’s behavior. I avoided doing a long list of evidence of bad behavior, because it is very unpleasant.


Note

Censoring and intimidation, way worse than Internet disagreement

I have no problem accepting disagreement. I understand that people will say things on Internet that I find unpleasant. But a strategy that relies on forcing removal of content from Internet create dishonest conversations. And an actor relying on intimidation and extorsion via legal procedures has no place in scientific or technical discussions.


Don’t let Valeriy lead smear campaigns

Valeriy will likely strike back, dedicating some of his communication energy to dismiss projects that I’m involved in.

Such a behavior actual does harm. We, human beings, are affected by propaganda, whether we are aware of it or not.

Please isolate Valeriy, unfollow him if you are following him, and do not relay his smear campaigns.

Do not hesitate to spread the present message if he runs smear campaigns or misbehave.

Do not hesitate to contact me if you want to bring forward more evidence of Valeriy’s bad behavior, so that we can build solid cases.
